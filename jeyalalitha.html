CM OF TAMILNADU

J.Jayalalithaa

2015-2016

J. Jayalalithaa, also known as Amma, was an Indian politician and actress who served as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu for six terms between 1991 and 2016. She was a prominent leader of the All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK) party and played a significant role in Tamil Nadu politics for over three decades. Born on February 24, 1948, in Mysore, Jayalalithaa started her career as an actress in Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada films. She entered politics in the 1980s and became a member of the AIADMK party in 1982. She rose through the ranks of the party quickly and became the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu for the first time in 1991. Jayalalithaa was known for her leadership skills and her advocacy of social welfare schemes. She introduced several initiatives aimed at promoting social welfare, including the Amma Unavagam, which provided affordable meals to the poor, and the Amma Kudineer Thittam, which aimed to provide clean drinking water to the people of Tamil Nadu. Jayalalithaa was a prolific politician and played a key role in Tamil Nadu politics for over three decades. She was widely regarded as a powerful leader and her contributions to the state and the nation were widely recognized. She passed away on December 5, 2016, at the age of 68.
