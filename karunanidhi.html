CM OF TAMILNADU

M.Karunanidhi

1996-2001

M. Karunanidhi, also known as Kalaignar, was an Indian politician and writer who served as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu for five terms between 1969 and 2011. He was a prominent leader of the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) party and played a significant role in Tamil Nadu politics for over six decades. Born on June 3, 1924, in Thirukuvalai village of Tamil Nadu, Karunanidhi started his career as a screenwriter and went on to become a prominent figure in Tamil cinema. He entered politics in the 1940s and became a member of the DMK party in 1949. Karunanidhi was known for his advocacy of Tamil nationalism and social justice. He introduced several social welfare schemes, including the Annadurai Memorial Housing Scheme, which aimed to provide affordable housing to low-income families. He was also instrumental in establishing the Tamil Nadu Open University, which aimed to provide educational opportunities to students from disadvantaged backgrounds. Karunanidhi was a prolific writer and authored several books in Tamil literature. He was also a renowned orator and was known for his powerful speeches. He was a key figure in the Dravidian movement, which aimed to promote Tamil nationalism and social reform. Karunanidhi passed away on August 7, 2018, at the age of 94. He was widely regarded as a towering figure in Tamil Nadu politics and his contributions to the state and the nation were widely recognized.
