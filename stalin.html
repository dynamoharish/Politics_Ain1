CM OF TAMILNADU

M.K.Stalin

2021-Present

M.K. Stalin, also known as Muthuvel Karunanidhi Stalin, is an Indian politician and the current Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu. He assumed office on May 7, 2021, following the victory of the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) party in the 2021 Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly election. Born on March 1, 1953, in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, Stalin is the son of M. Karunanidhi, a former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu and the leader of the DMK party. Stalin began his political career as a member of the DMK in the 1970s and has served as a member of the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly since 1989. Stalin is known for his focus on social welfare schemes and his advocacy of development. He has introduced several initiatives aimed at promoting social welfare, including the Kudimaramathu Scheme, which aims to rejuvenate water bodies in Tamil Nadu, and the Uzhavan App, which aims to connect farmers with government schemes and markets. Stalin's contributions to Tamil Nadu and Indian politics are widely recognized. He has played a significant role in the DMK party and has been instrumental in the party's success in the 2021 Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly election.
