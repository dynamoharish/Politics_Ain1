CM OF TAMILNADU

M.G.Ramachandran

1977-1987

M. G. Ramachandran, popularly known as MGR, was an Indian actor and politician who served as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu for three terms between 1977 and 1987. He was a prominent leader of the All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK) party and played a significant role in Tamil Nadu politics for over two decades. Born on January 17, 1917, in Kandy, Sri Lanka, MGR started his career as an actor in Tamil cinema. He gained popularity for his roles in films that portrayed him as a champion of the poor and oppressed. He entered politics in the 1960s and became a member of the DMK party. He left the DMK in 1972 and founded the AIADMK party. MGR was known for his charisma and his advocacy of social welfare schemes. He introduced several initiatives aimed at promoting social welfare, including the Mid-Day Meal Scheme, which provided free meals to school children, and the Annadurai Memorial Housing Scheme, which aimed to provide affordable housing to low-income families. MGR was widely regarded as a powerful leader and his contributions to Tamil Nadu were widely recognized. He passed away on December 24, 1987, at the age of 70. His legacy continues to inspire millions of people in Tamil Nadu, and he remains a revered figure in Tamil Nadu politics.
