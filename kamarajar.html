CM OF TAMILNADU

K.Kamarajar

1954-1963

K. Kamaraj, also known as Perunthalaivar Kamarajar, was an Indian politician and freedom fighter who served as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu for two terms between 1954 and 1963. He was a prominent leader of the Indian National Congress party and played a significant role in Indian politics for over three decades. Born on July 15, 1903, in Virudhunagar district of Tamil Nadu, Kamaraj dropped out of school at a young age to support his family. He was actively involved in the Indian independence movement and was imprisoned several times by the British authorities. He later served as the President of the Tamil Nadu Congress Committee and played a key role in the Indian independence movement. Kamaraj was known for his simplicity and his advocacy of education and social welfare schemes. He introduced several initiatives aimed at promoting education, including the Kamaraj Plan, which aimed to provide free education to all children up to the age of 14. He was also instrumental in establishing several schools and colleges in Tamil Nadu. Kamaraj was widely regarded as a selfless leader and his contributions to Tamil Nadu and India were widely recognized. He passed away on October 2, 1975, at the age of 72. His legacy continues to inspire millions of people in Tamil Nadu and he remains a revered figure in Indian politics.
