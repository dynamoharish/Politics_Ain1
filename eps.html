CM OF TAMILNADU

Edappadi K.Palaniswami

2017-2021

Edappadi K. Palaniswami, also known as EPS, is an Indian politician and the current Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu. He was sworn in as Chief Minister on February 16, 2017, following the resignation of O. Panneerselvam. Born on May 12, 1954, in Salem district of Tamil Nadu, EPS started his political career as a member of the AIADMK party. He was first elected to the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly in 1989 and has been a member of the assembly for several terms since then. EPS served as the Minister for Highways and Minor Ports in the Tamil Nadu government between 2011 and 2016. He was appointed as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu in 2017 following the death of J. Jayalalithaa, the former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu and the leader of the AIADMK party. EPS is known for his focus on development and his advocacy of social welfare schemes. He has introduced several initiatives aimed at promoting social welfare, including the Amma Two Wheeler Scheme, which provides subsidized two-wheelers to women in Tamil Nadu, and the Amma Unavagam, which provides affordable meals to the poor. EPS continues to play a significant role in Tamil Nadu politics and his contributions to the state are widely recognized.
